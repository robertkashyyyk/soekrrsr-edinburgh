Still loose — possibilities on the table

Match day

Premier Sports Cup quarter-final

A QF could fall on this weekend — the tie isn't set until the earlier rounds finish, so who's playing and where is still open. If it's Rangers at Ibrox (Glasgow) or Hearts at Tynecastle (Edinburgh), it slots in easily. Watch for the draw and grab tickets the moment it's confirmed — they go fast.

Suzanne's shout

Climb Arthur's Seat

The old volcano above Holyrood Park — a proper little climb to the best view over Edinburgh. About an hour up at a steady pace; pairs naturally with a day in town.

Make something

Handbag workshop · Islander Harris Tweed

On the Royal Mile at Islander Harris Tweed — pick your cloth and make your own handbag to take home. Worth booking ahead once we settle on a day.

Old Town

Edinburgh Castle

The one everyone means — up on the rock above Princes Street: the crown jewels, the One O'Clock Gun, big views over the city. Pre-book a timed ticket (it's busy) and fold it into an Edinburgh day.

One evening

Ghost tour — or the comedy one

An evening walk through the Old Town's closes and vaults, leaning to the ghostly end. The Mountebank-style comedy walking tour is the cheerier alternative — we'd do one or the other, and the spooky one's winning.

Down at Leith

Royal Yacht Britannia

The Queen's former royal yacht, berthed at Ocean Terminal in Leith — a self-guided wander across five decks with the tea room on board. Half a day, and easy to pair with a mooch round Leith.

A day's expedition

The Loch Ness monster day

A proper Highland day out, chasing the beast. Be honest about the distance, though: Loch Ness sits about three hours' drive north of Aberdour (~150 miles up the A9), so it claims a whole day — an early-ish start and an evening return. The drive through the Cairngorms is half the reward.

The classic stretch is the Urquhart Castle end near Drumnadrochit — a ruined castle on a headland over the loch's deepest, most-photographed water. Pair the castle with a boat and you've got the day. Three to weigh up:

Our pick

Castle Cruises Loch Ness

Temple Pier, Drumnadrochit
~1 hr · 12 seats

Small skippered boat right under the castle — the closest and most personal. Tiny, so book ahead. lochnesscruises.com →

Loch Ness by Jacobite

Nr Inverness
1–3½ hr · big boats

The big, slick operator. Combo tickets bundle the cruise with Urquhart Castle and the visitor centre.

Cruise Loch Ness

Fort Augustus (south)
~50 min · live sonar

South-end departure with sonar screens on board — handy if we come at the loch from that side.
